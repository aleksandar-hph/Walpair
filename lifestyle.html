LIFESTYLE

Life & Style: Jamie Eionto

Explore

The Chalk Games star reveals life on the road, her budding kitty romance, and her secret for living in the moment.

By Norman Ridgewick

Q. This year you’ve had a busy schedule filming two movies, and the release of your book. How do you keep up with it all?

I love what I’m doing, and I think that makes it easier for me to keep going. Acting just feels natural to me. If I took myself too seriously then I think I would start to feel burned out. But I’m just keeping it real, just going out there and enjoying it. The book tour has been so much fun, it allows me to connect a lot more with my fans. You don’t get that at five a.m. on the set. It’s been great to meet them and get to share my story with them.

Q. What is your favorite thing to do on a Saturday night?

When I’m filming it doesn’t matter what day of the week it is, I’m in bed early. Sometimes I’ll work for three weeks straight and then get three days off. When I am off it’s all about relaxation. Sometimes I’ll go to my favorite day spa, and sometimes I’ll just stay in and order pizza. It just depends on how I feel. When I’m not filming I like to go out with my friends and go dancing or catch a movie. Whatever moves me in the moment.

Q. Where do you find your inspiration?

My mom is a huge inspiration to me. She raised four kids and worked full time, and somehow she always managed to find time for us. She worked really hard and never gave up. Anytime I’m feeling a little burned out I think about how she use to handle the four of us, and we were a handful, yet she was still smiling at the end of the day. She did the hard work, what I do is easy in comparison.

Q. So are there any plans in your future to get married and have kids in your near future?

Right now I’m completely focused on my acting. But if Mr. Right came along, then of course, one day I’d like to get married and have children. I’m in no rush, having children is a huge decision and I want to make sure that when I step into that chapter in my life it’s the perfect time. For now I have my new kitty, Kiko to keep me happy. She’s the sweetest little cuddle bug!

Q. What do you wish to accomplish in the next five years?

Well, I have a new movie in the works. We’ll start recording in a few months. I have a six more dates for my book tour coming up, and then we’ll see what the future holds. I am entertaining the idea of appearing on a sitcom, but it’s in the early stages, and I don’t know if it’ll pan out yet. I’m really just taking it all one day at a time.
